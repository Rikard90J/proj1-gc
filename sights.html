Love Gran Canaria

Gran Canaria has many beautiful beaches where you can experience the crystal clear water of the Atlantic Ocean. The temperature normally stays around 20-30 degrees celsius all year round and the south side of the island has as many as 300 days of sun each year. The island has two different climate zones. The north side of the island is colder and more humid than the south side which has a warmer and drier subtropical climate.

Sights

Firgas

In the northern part of Gran Canaria is the city of Firgas. It is a beautiful and quaint town known for producing mineral water.

Arucas

The central part of this is both historically and culturally important. Here you can find the beautiful church of San Juan Bautista.

Agaete

This city in the north west of the island is home to exciting archeological finds. One example is the cemetery Maípes.

Las Canteras

One of Europes most popular beaches can actually be found on Gran Canaria: Las Canteras. It is more than 2 kilometres long and therefore perfect for nice walks.
